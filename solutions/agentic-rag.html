Under federal regulation, direct procurement (without a competitive process) is permitted for contracts up to AED 200,000, raised from AED 150,000 in the 2024 amendments¹. Procurement officers must still document a single-source justification.

Dubai's emirate-level rules diverge in two ways²³:

  • The threshold is set lower at AED 100,000 for departments under Dubai Government Excellence Programme.
  • Above that, framework-agreement procurement is available for repeat purchases of routine supplies, which the federal rules do not codify.

For cross-jurisdictional procurement, the stricter ceiling applies — the federal rule only sets the upper bound⁴.

Common questions

The things every CIO asks.

If yours isn't here, ask us directly — we welcome the security questionnaire.

Send your questionnaire
Does our data ever leave our perimeter?

No. The default deployment is on-prem or sovereign-cloud, single-tenant, with all model weights, embeddings and source documents resident inside your boundary. There is no required outbound call. For air-gapped clients we deliver a fully offline release artefact.

Which model do you use? Can we bring our own?

By default we deploy open-weight models (Llama 3, Qwen, Mistral families) — they are competitive on retrieval-heavy workloads and they run inside your perimeter. Clients can also bring their own model: Azure OpenAI in a private link, an internal fine-tune, or a sovereign endpoint. The agent runtime is model-agnostic.

How do you prevent hallucinations?

Three layers. First, the retrieval is good — strong recall on the right document. Second, the agent self-verifies: every claim in the draft is checked against its cited source before the answer is shown. Third, the UI never hides the source — users see the chunk, not just the citation. On benchmarks our hallucination rate sits below 1%.

How are document permissions handled?

Permissions are inherited from the source system at ingest time and stored at the chunk level. Every retrieval is filtered by the asking user's effective permissions before the model sees anything. The model can never retrieve a chunk the user is not already entitled to read.

What about prompt injection from uploaded documents?

We treat all retrieved text as untrusted. Tool execution is sandboxed; tool calls require structured arguments, not free-form prompts. Documents are scanned for instruction-override patterns at ingest. Sensitive tools (email, ticket creation, code execution) require explicit user confirmation in the UI.

Can we audit what the model said and saw?

Yes — every session is replayable. Compliance officers see the question, the retrieval results (including filtered-out items), every tool call and its arguments, the draft, the verified-claim mapping, and the final answer. Audit ledger is append-only with 7-year retention by default.

What's pricing?

A platform subscription based on user-seats and corpus size, plus a one-time deployment fee. Sovereign / air-gapped contracts are quoted separately because the support model is different. We share a worked example in the discovery call — no obligation.
